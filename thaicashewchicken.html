Thai Cashew Chicken

  • Hot Thai Kitchen

    • Thai Cashew Chicken, known locally as Gai Pad Med Mamuang, is a flavorful stir-fry that combines tender pieces of chicken with crunchy roasted cashew nuts, colorful vegetables like onions, bell peppers, and dried chilies, all tossed in a savory-sweet sauce. The dish balances the richness of cashews with the umami of soy sauce, oyster sauce, and a hint of sweetness, while the chilies add a gentle heat without overpowering the flavor. It is loved for its contrast in textures—juicy chicken, crisp vegetables, and crunchy nuts—making it both satisfying and easy to enjoy. Popular in Thai restaurants worldwide, Thai Cashew Chicken is a perfect example of how Thai cuisine blends simple ingredients into a dish that is wholesome, aromatic, and deeply flavorful.

    Ingredients

    • Chicken 400 grams
    • Cashew Nuts 200 grams
    • Bell peppers 150 grams
    • Onion 150 grams
    • Garlic 20 grams
    • Green Onion 50 grams
    • Dried Chilli 10 grams
    • Frying Flour 3-4 tablespoons
    • A handful of salt
    • Black pepper 1/4 tablespoon
    • Chili paste 1/2 tablespoon
    • Oyster sauce 1 tablespoon
    • Soy sauce 1/2 tablespoon
    • Sugar 2/3 tablespoon

    How to cook

    1. Clean the chicken in water and take it out to chop it into pieces, then soak it in the frying flour and make sure the pieces are all covered in the flour
    2. Set up the frying pan with oil and fry the chicken until yellow and and take them out and when the oil dries fry it again
    3. Pour out the used oil and leave some for stir frying, then put in the spices, sauces and sugar and stir all of them and then taste
    4. Put in the fried chicken to stir fry and when the sauce covers the chicken put int the Green onions, bell peppers, dried chili and cashew nuts to finally stir them all together then turn off the gas and it is ready to eat
