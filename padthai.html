Pad Thai

  • Hot Thai Kitchen

    • Pad Thai is Thailand’s most iconic noodle dish, celebrated for its perfect harmony of sweet, sour, salty, and savory flavors. It is made with stir-fried rice noodles, eggs, tofu or shrimp, bean sprouts, and chives, tossed in a tangy sauce of tamarind paste, fish sauce, palm sugar, and chili. Traditionally garnished with crushed peanuts, lime wedges, and sometimes dried chili flakes, it offers layers of texture and flavor in every bite. Light yet filling, Pad Thai is both street food and restaurant favorite, symbolizing Thailand’s culinary creativity and its ability to transform simple ingredients into a globally loved dish.

    Ingredients

    • 12 shrimp
    • 150 grams Pad Thai noodles
    • 1 handful bean sprouts
    • 2 eggs
    • 1 tablespoon dried shrimp
    • 1 tablespoon peanuts
    • 1 tablespoon preserved radish
    • 2 handfuls tofu
    • 3 stalks Chinese chives
    • 3 shallots
    • 120 grams chili sauce
    • 70 grams tomato sauce
    • 150 grams palm sugar
    • 45 grams fish sauce
    • 120 grams tamarind juice
    • 1 teaspoon seasoning powder
    • 80 grams water

    How to cook

    1. Heat oil in a pan and fry the shallots for a few minutes. Add palm sugar, fish sauce, tamarind juice, water, and chili sauce. Stir to combine. Continue simmering for a few minutes until the sauce is cooked through. Turn off the heat and set aside.
    2. Heat a wok and add oil and shrimp stock. Stir-fry until cooked, then remove from heat.
    3. Add thin rice noodles (as much as desired) to the wok. Add a little water. Add preserved radish, yellow tofu, peanuts, and dried shrimp. Stir-fry until combined, then move the noodles to the side. Crack an egg into the wok and scramble until cooked. Add the noodles and stir-fry together. Add bean sprouts and chives. Stir-fry for a few minutes, then remove from the wok and set aside.
